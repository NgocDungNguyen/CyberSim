Spoofing Attack

Overview

Technical Explanation

Spoofing is a type of cyber attack where an attacker disguises themselves as a trusted source to gain unauthorized access to systems, steal data, or spread malware. This can involve falsifying data to hide the sender's identity, routing traffic through proxy servers, or using a trusted IP address to bypass access controls. Common types include IP spoofing, email spoofing, and DNS spoofing.

Real-life Metaphor

Imagine you're at a masquerade ball where everyone wears masks. A person wearing a mask of your best friend approaches you and asks for your house keys. You might be inclined to trust them because they look and sound like your friend. However, it's actually an impostor taking advantage of the disguise. Spoofing works similarly in the digital world: attackers disguise themselves as trusted entities to trick you into granting access or sharing sensitive information.
