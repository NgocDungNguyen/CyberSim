Spoofing Attack

Overview

What is Spoofing?

Spoofing is when someone pretends to be someone else online. They might:

  • Fake an email address
  • Pretend to be a trusted website
  • Use a fake phone number
The goal is to trick you into sharing information or doing something you shouldn't.

Real-life Example

Imagine you're at a costume party. Someone wearing a mask of your friend asks for your house keys. You might give them because they look like your friend.

Spoofing is similar: attackers disguise themselves as trusted sources to trick you online.

Real-World Spoofing Attack Case Studies

Brazilian Bank DNS Spoofing (2017)

A massive DNS spoofing attack affected multiple Brazilian banks and their customers.

  • Attackers compromised DNS servers of major Brazilian ISPs
  • Bank domain names were redirected to cloned phishing websites
  • Affected potentially millions of Brazilian internet users

Impact: Exposed sensitive financial information of countless users and damaged trust in online banking systems.

Lithuanian Man's $100 Million Email Spoofing Scam (2019)

A Lithuanian man was charged for a sophisticated email spoofing attack against two major tech companies.

  • The attacker sent spoofed emails impersonating a legitimate Asian hardware vendor
  • Convinced the companies to wire over $100 million to bank accounts he controlled
  • Used forged invoices, contracts, and letters to add credibility

Impact: Resulted in the theft of $100 million from major tech companies, highlighting the financial risks of email spoofing.

Caller ID Spoofing in Vishing Attacks (Ongoing)

Widespread use of caller ID spoofing in voice phishing (vishing) attacks.

  • Attackers spoof caller IDs to appear as legitimate banks, government agencies, or tech support
  • Victims are more likely to trust and engage with the caller
  • Often combined with social engineering tactics to extract sensitive information

Impact: Ongoing threat leading to financial losses and identity theft for numerous individuals.

IP Spoofing in DDoS Attacks (Various Incidents)

IP spoofing is frequently used to amplify and obfuscate the source of Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks.

  • Attackers send requests with spoofed source IP addresses to reflection servers
  • Reflection servers send amplified responses to the victim's IP address
  • Makes it difficult to trace the attack back to its true source

Impact: Has led to numerous high-profile website outages and service disruptions across various industries.
