Ransomware

Overview

Technical Explanation

Ransomware is a type of malicious software (malware) that encrypts a victim's files. The attacker then demands a ransom from the victim to restore access to the data upon payment. Users are shown instructions for how to pay a fee to get the decryption key. The costs can range from a few hundred dollars to thousands, payable to cybercriminals in cryptocurrency.

Real-life Metaphor

Imagine coming home to find that all your possessions have been locked in an impenetrable safe. A stranger contacts you, claiming they have the only key, and demands payment for its return. This is similar to how ransomware operates, locking away your digital possessions (files) and demanding payment for their release.

Real-World Ransomware Attack Case Studies

WannaCry (2017)

A global ransomware attack that affected over 200,000 computers across 150 countries.

  • Exploited a vulnerability in Microsoft Windows
  • Encrypted files and demanded payment in Bitcoin
  • Caused an estimated $4 billion in damages globally

Impact: Disrupted critical services, including healthcare systems in the UK's National Health Service.

Colonial Pipeline Attack (2021)

A ransomware attack that targeted the largest fuel pipeline in the United States.

  • Caused by a single compromised password
  • Led to a shutdown of the pipeline for several days
  • Resulted in fuel shortages and panic buying in the southeastern U.S.

Impact: The company paid a $4.4 million ransom and the attack highlighted vulnerabilities in critical infrastructure.

Kaseya VSA Attack (2021)

A supply chain ransomware attack that affected managed service providers and their clients.

  • Exploited vulnerabilities in Kaseya's VSA software
  • Impacted over 1,500 businesses worldwide
  • Attackers demanded $70 million in ransom

Impact: Demonstrated the far-reaching consequences of supply chain attacks in the IT industry.

Baltimore City Ransomware Attack (2019)

A ransomware attack that crippled Baltimore's city government systems for weeks.

  • Affected various city services, including property transactions and water bills
  • Attackers demanded $76,000 in Bitcoin, which the city refused to pay
  • Recovery efforts cost an estimated $18 million

Impact: Highlighted the vulnerability of local governments to ransomware attacks and the high cost of recovery.
