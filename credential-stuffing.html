Credential Stuffing

Overview

Technical Explanation

Credential stuffing is a type of cyberattack where attackers use stolen account credentials from one service to attempt to log in to other services. This attack exploits the common practice of users reusing the same username and password across multiple sites.

Real-life Metaphor

Imagine you have a master key that opens your house, car, and office. If someone steals this key, they can now access all these places. Similarly, when users use the same password for multiple accounts, a breach in one service can compromise many others.

Real-World Credential Stuffing Case Studies

Dunkin' Donuts (2018)

In 2018, Dunkin' Donuts faced a credential stuffing attack that targeted its DD Perks rewards program members.

  • Attackers used credentials from other data breaches to access customer accounts.
  • They could potentially access names, email addresses, DD Perks account numbers, and QR codes.
  • Dunkin' Donuts forced password resets for affected accounts and encouraged users to use unique passwords.

Impact: Potential theft of personal information and loyalty points.

State Farm Insurance (2019)

In August 2019, State Farm Insurance disclosed that it had been the target of a credential stuffing attack.

  • Attackers attempted to access customer accounts using login credentials obtained from other sources.
  • State Farm detected the attack through its monitoring systems.
  • The company reset passwords for affected accounts and notified customers.

Impact: Potential unauthorized access to personal information, though no fraudulent activity was reported.

Disney+ Launch (2019)

Shortly after its launch in November 2019, Disney+ users reported their accounts being hacked and sold on the dark web.

  • Thousands of Disney+ accounts were compromised within hours of the service's launch.
  • Attackers used email and password combinations from previous data breaches.
  • Some users reported being locked out of their accounts.

Impact: Unauthorized access to accounts, potential exposure of personal and payment information.

Canadian Government (2020)

In August 2020, the Canadian government reported a series of credential stuffing attacks on its GCKey service and Canada Revenue Agency.

  • Approximately 11,200 accounts were compromised in the attacks.
  • Attackers used passwords and usernames from previous data breaches.
  • Some accounts were used to fraudulently apply for government COVID-19 relief benefits.

Impact: Unauthorized access to government services, potential identity theft, and fraudulent benefit claims.
