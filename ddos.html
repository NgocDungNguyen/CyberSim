DDoS Attacks

Overview

Technical Explanation

A Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack is a malicious attempt to disrupt the normal traffic of a targeted server, service, or network by overwhelming it with a flood of internet traffic. This is achieved by using multiple compromised systems as sources of attack traffic, often forming a botnet.

Real-life Metaphor

Imagine a store with a single entrance. If a huge crowd of people blocks the entrance, legitimate customers cannot enter the store. Similarly, in a DDoS attack, the server is overwhelmed with fake traffic, preventing legitimate users from accessing it.
